1.

Reasons for cases and their contributions:

The trend focuses on reasons for cases such as work, family, and mental health-related issues. Trends are displayed based on years and months. Users can adjust the years and months to view the data, which is presented visually through bar graphs showing reasons and their contribution percentages. Each graph represents a year with reasons on the x-axis and case time on the y-axis.

2.

Hospitals with the highest total cases due to operational issues:

This trend identifies hospitals with the longest total cases due to economic issues in a selected year. Users can select the year to view data, displayed visually through a line graph showing monthly total case times.

3.

Cases due to poor work conditions in a city:

This trend analyzes cases due to poor work conditions in a city during a specific year. Users can select the city and year for trend analysis. Visualization includes graphs showing monthly case times and reasons for poor work conditions.

4.

Cities with the highest cases during each season:

This trend highlights cities experiencing the highest cases during each season. Users can select the season for visualization, which is presented as a bar graph showing cities on the x-axis and case times on the y-axis.

5.

Hospitals with the highest number of cases due to family issues:

This trend identifies the top five hospitals with the highest number of family-related cases in a selected month and year. Visualization includes a bar graph with hospitals on the x-axis and total cases on the y-axis.
